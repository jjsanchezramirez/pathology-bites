## Strategic Notes

### **Prioritization Strategy**
- **Safety-first approach**: Emphasized transfusion reactions, compatibility testing, and adverse events
- **Clinical relevance over designation**: Selected high-yield AR topics (HIT, TRALI, TA-GVHD) over some routine Core items
- **Practical focus**: Concentrated on scenarios pathologists encounter in practice vs. theoretical knowledge

### **Major High-Yield Areas Emphasized**
- **Transfusion reactions** (acute hemolytic, TRALI, TACO, TA-GVHD): Critical for patient safety
- **Compatibility testing and antibody work**: Daily practice essentials
- **Coagulation disorders and platelet issues**: High-frequency clinical problems  
- **Apheresis indications**: TTP, therapeutic plasma exchange, red cell exchange
- **Pediatric/obstetric**: HDFN, neonatal transfusion, maternal hemorrhage

### **Strategic Exclusions**
- **Fellow-level content**: HPC processing, advanced cellular therapy
- **Rare entities**: Exotic blood group variants, uncommon parasites
- **Pure technical procedures**: Detailed equipment operation, basic processing
- **Outdated practices**: Historical testing methods no longer in use

### **Educational Efficiency Focus**
- **Combined differential questions**: Warm vs. cold AIHA, TACO vs. TRALI
- **Multi-system integration**: Massive transfusion incorporating trauma, coagulation, and blood components
- **Practical decision-making**: When to use plasma vs. factors, platelet thresholds, emergency protocols

### **Blueprint Adherence**
- **Clinical Practice** heavily weighted (15 questions) reflecting high clinical impact
- **Management topics** essential for medical directors and quality oversight
- **Infectious hazards** focused on current threats and prevention strategies
- **Specialty areas** (apheresis, transplant) appropriately represented but not over-emphasized
