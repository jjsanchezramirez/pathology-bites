## Strategic Notes

### Educational Objective Framework
Each question emphasizes **essential pediatric pathologic competencies** that general pathology residents encounter:

**Tumor Recognition Objectives:**
- "Recognize Wilms tumor morphology and apply staging criteria for treatment planning"
- "Classify neuroblastoma morphologic patterns and correlate with prognosis"
- "Distinguish rhabdomyosarcoma subtypes and correlate with molecular markers"

**Congenital Anomaly Objectives:**
- "Classify congenital heart defects and understand hemodynamic consequences"
- "Recognize aganglionosis and apply diagnostic criteria for Hirschsprung disease"
- "Classify neural tube defects and understand developmental mechanisms"

**GI/Liver/Inflammatory Objectives:**
- "Recognize celiac disease morphology and understand diagnostic criteria in pediatric patients"
- "Identify eosinophilic gastroenteritis patterns and correlate with allergic history"
- "Recognize autoimmune hepatitis features and distinguish from other pediatric liver diseases"

**Soft Tissue Tumor Objectives:**
- "Recognize nodular fasciitis morphology and distinguish from sarcoma"
- "Identify granular cell tumor morphology and understand clinical behavior"
- "Distinguish lipoblastoma from liposarcoma in pediatric patients"

**Developmental Disorder Objectives:**
- "Identify hyaline membrane disease and correlate with surfactant deficiency"
- "Grade intraventricular hemorrhage and correlate with gestational age"
- "Understand SIDS diagnostic criteria and differential diagnosis"

**Comprehensive Tumor Coverage** (30%):

- Included both common (Wilms tumor) and rare but important (rhabdoid tumor) entities
- Balanced organ system representation

**Essential Congenital Anomalies** (17%):
- Major malformations affecting multiple organ systems
- Emphasized surgical and management implications
- Included both isolated and syndromic anomalies
- Focused on entities requiring pathologic confirmation

**Expanded Soft Tissue Coverage** (14%):

- Balanced common and rare entities

**GI/Liver/Inflammatory Disorders** (10%):
