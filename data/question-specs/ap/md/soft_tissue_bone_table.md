## Strategic Notes

### Major Inclusions & Rationale
- **All major sarcoma types prioritized** - liposarcoma, leiomyosarcoma, synovial sarcoma, Ewing sarcoma represent the most critical soft tissue malignancies
- **Emphasized benign vs. malignant differentials** - these represent the highest-stakes diagnostic decisions in musculoskeletal pathology
- **Major bone tumors comprehensively covered** - osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma, giant cell tumor, metastatic disease are essential competencies
- **Critical mimics included** - reactive lesions simulating sarcomas is essential to prevent overdiagnosis

### Major Exclusions & Rationale
- **Normal anatomy/histology** - standard exclusions per guidelines
- **Rare sarcoma subtypes** - alveolar soft parts sarcoma, desmoplastic small round cell tumor excluded due to extreme rarity
- **Lymphomas in bone** - belong in hematopathology section, not musculoskeletal
- **Some advanced AR entities** - chondroblastoma, adamantinoma excluded for higher-yield content
- **Basic metabolic conditions** - osteoporosis, osteomalacia excluded as more medical than pathologic diagnoses

### Educational Efficiency Strategies
- **Combined benign-malignant differentials** - each malignant entity paired with its benign counterpart for maximum learning
- **Focused on immunohistochemical confirmation** - emphasized diagnostic markers that clinicians actually use
- **Pattern recognition emphasized** - morphologic features that distinguish entities in practical scenarios
- **Molecular correlation included** - synovial sarcoma translocations, clear cell sarcoma genetics for contemporary practice
