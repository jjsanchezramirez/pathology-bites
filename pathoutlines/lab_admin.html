Question 1


Using the provided dataset for precision studies for a hemoglobin A1c assay, which of the following statements is true?

  1. A positive shift in both levels is observed on day 14
  2. Level 1 quality control shows more consistency and stable performance compared to level 2
  3. Quality control material is inappropriate for precision studies
  4. The total allowable error of 6% is too stringent and should be widened
Answer 1
A. A positive shift in both levels is observed on day 14. Overall, precision studies show adequate performance for both levels of quality control material in the 20 x 2 x 2 format and are acceptable. Level 2 quality control shows a smaller CV and more consistency than level 1. A total allowable error of 6% is too wide as a precision goal for hemoglobin a1c, which has relatively small absolute values.

Question 2


Using the provided dataset for method comparison studies for a hemoglobin A1c assay, which of the following statements is true?

  1. The data shows acceptable linearity
  2. The result suggests analytical errors due to carryover
  3. There is an increasing negative bias of the new method with increasing HbA1c concentrations
  4. There is an insufficient number of data points for the analysis to be conclusive
Answer 2
C. While the average bias of -1.5% is within the total allowable error, it is clear that there is an increasing negative bias of the new method with increasing HbA1c concentrations (current method is negatively biased against new method). The bias hovers around 6% starting around 6.0% A1c units and greater, which encompasses the medical decision limit of 6.5% A1c units. This bias renders the method comparison result not acceptable for patient testing.

Question 3
When is the best time to prepare a succession plan for a department or a group of pathologists?

  1. Any time a new position in the group opens
  2. At the time of a pathologist's retirement
  3. Well in advance of needing one as it is part of a group's overall staffing strategy
  4. When pathologists start complaining of burnout
Answer 3
C. Well in advance of needing one as it is part of a group's overall staffing strategy. A succession plan is never an ad hoc activity. It needs to be designed and implemented a long time in advance of a staffing vacancy. Answer A is incorrect because a succession plan needs to be in place before a new position becomes available as it may take up to 18 - 24 months to fill it. Answer B is incorrect because pathologists' retirement plans should be shared with the group's leadership so that succession planning can happen well in advance of such plans. Answer D is incorrect because if burnout happens, it may be indicative, among other reasons, of the absence of a succession plan or of a poorly designed and executed one.

Question 4
What is the best next step after a succession plan has been established for a department or group of pathologists?

  1. Lock it in a safe and keep it secret so that nobody can see it
  2. Say that no such plan exists if asked about it
  3. Share it with select members of the department you are close to
  4. Share it with relevant stakeholders
Answer 4
D. Share it with relevant stakeholders. A succession plan is a strategic document that needs to be shared with all relevant stakeholders so that they can prepare and adjust their staffing strategies accordingly. Answer A is incorrect because locking a succession plan in a safe and keeping it secret defeats the purpose of having such a critically important strategic document in the first place. Answer C is incorrect because the plan should be shared with all critical stakeholders, not just those you are close to. Answer B is incorrect because hiding the truth is never a good idea.

Question 5
Which of the following represents the maximum amount of money that can be collected from all sources (insurance and patient) for a laboratory procedure?

  1. Contractual adjustment
  2. Contractual amount
  3. Fee schedule charge
  4. Gross charge
  5. Payment received from insurance payer
Answer 5
B. The contractual amount is the amount that the provider, based on contracted agreement, has agreed to accept for the service provided. It includes the amount that the insurance company will pay and the amount that is to be collected as a copay from the patient.

The gross charge, which is also the fee schedule charge, is typically set high to ensure maximum collection from any payer and does not reflect what will ultimately be received. Payment from the insurance payer may reflect a significant portion of the contractual amount; however, the patient must be billed for the difference. Contractual adjustment is the difference between the gross charge and the contractual amount, reflecting effectively a discount off of the gross charge.

Question 8
Which of the following scenarios is least likely to benefit from a root cause analysis?

  1. Addressing the cause of blood product administration to the wrong patient
  2. Daily monitoring of personnel punctuality to ensure adequacy of shift coverage
  3. Explaining the reason for difficulties in personnel recruitment and retention
  4. Increased turnaround time for surgical pathology reports
  5. Investigating a complaint regarding frequent specimen mislabeling
Answer 8
B. Daily monitoring of personnel punctuality to ensure adequacy of shift coverage. RCA is most practical when used as an investigative tool to uncover and address the chief underlying reason(s) for an undesired event, trend or outcome. It has less utility as a proactive surveillance tool. The daily monitoring of personnel punctuality to ensure adequacy of shift coverage is an ongoing surveillance activity; unless a negative trend is discovered, RCA is not likely to be directly applicable. The underlying reason (root cause) for increased turnaround time in surgical pathology, specimen mislabeling or incorrect blood product administration may originate from systemic issues beyond what may be immediately visible and thus these scenarios may benefit from RCAs. Similarly, difficulties in personnel recruitment and retention may be due to reasons other than labor market forces (such as institutional / departmental reputation, conflicting personalities, safety concerns, onboarding problems, human resource conflicts) and as such, may benefit from RCA.

Reference: Causal analysis

Question 12


Reference interval Sample size
Ceruloplasmin (mg/L) 159 - 414 603

A group of investigators from Saudi Arabia was studying the reference intervals of serum ceruloplasmin in adult healthy individuals. The results of their study are shown above. How many of the 603 subjects are expected to have a ceruloplasmin concentration of greater than 454 mg/L?

  1. 3
  2. 6
  3. 9
  4. 12
Answer 12
A. 3. The data show a normal distribution; mean: 286.5. By definition, reference interval = mean ± 1.96 SD → 1 SD = 65. Ceruloplasmin levels of more than 454 will be approximately 2.58 SD from the mean / median (454 - 286.5 = 167.5 = 2.58 x 65 = 2.58 SD). 99% of values will be between (mean - 2.58 SD) and (mean + 2.58 SD), 0.5% values will be < (mean - 2.58 SD) and 0.5% values will be > (mean + 2.58 SD). Number of values > 454 will be 0.5% x 603 (n) = 3.

Reference: Validation of reference intervals and reportable range

Question 16


A 51 year old woman presented at a community clinic with an infected wound on her foot that she reports having for ~1 month.   2. Education: access and quality

Answer C is incorrect because access to healthcare was not the primary issue in this scenario. Answer D is incorrect because while neighborhood and built environment could be a contributing factor (the prescription might be available at a lower cost at a different location), it is not the primary factor here. Answer E is incorrect because while social and community support in this case could help, it is not directly related to cost.

Question 17


A 51 year old woman presented at a community clinic with an infected wound on her foot that she reports having for ~1 month. If she was unable to get the medicine that she needed due to lack of transportation to the pharmacy, which Healthy People 2030 social determinant of health would primarily account for this healthcare shortcoming?

  1. Economic stability
  2. Education: access and quality
  3. Healthcare: access and quality
  4. Neighborhood and built environment
  5. Social and community context
Answer 17
D. Neighborhood and built environment. To some, the lack of transportation to and from the pharmacy may seem like a small issue but it can be a primary reason for medication noncompliance. Built environment issues, such as the lack of a pharmacy within walking distance or safety issues along a route to a pharmacy, can account for the inability to fill a prescription and return home safely. Adequate access to a pharmacy within a reasonable distance on public transit (or safe delivery options) can also be a burden. In this scenario, while all other options listed could contribute to transportation being the issue and are likely partially correct depending on circumstance, answer D is the best option in general.

Answer A is incorrect because while economic instability does affect access to transportation, it is not a direct cause here. Answer B is incorrect because while education level might provide the opportunity to earn a higher wage and the ability to live in close proximity to services, it is not directly related to lack of transportation. Answer C is incorrect because access to healthcare was not the primary issue in this scenario. Answer E is incorrect because while social and community support could help in this case, the burden of lack of access to a pharmacy likely affects the entire community and relying on others for transportation may not be tenable long term.

Question 18


A 51 year old woman presented at a community clinic with an infected wound on her foot that she reports having for ~1 month. She states that 3 years ago she was seen at the clinic for excessive fatigue and blurry vision and chart consultation indicates that she was diagnosed with diabetes mellitus. At that time, she was given insulin but says that once it ran out, she did not get more.

If this patient did not get the medicine because she did not know that she had a chronic condition and thought it would resolve on its own, which Healthy People 2030 social determinant of health could primarily account for this healthcare shortcoming?

  1. Economic stability
  2. Education: access and quality
  3. Healthcare: access and quality
  4. Neighborhood and built environment
  5. Social and community context
Answer 18
B. Education: access and quality. Health education and the learned ability to understand one's own health status is the likely issue in this scenario. Reported barriers to health education, primarily in lower income communities include limited English proficiency, lack of healthcare related practical skills (e.g., injections), lack of access or understanding of medical information and community misinformation. Any of these barriers could have contributed to this patient's misunderstanding of her disease status.

Answers A, D and E are incorrect because they are indirectly causative in this case; however, they are likely partially correct depending on circumstance. Answer C is incorrect because access to healthcare was not the primary issue in this scenario; however, low quality healthcare and inadequate information from the provider may have contributed to this case. Community health clinics can be overburdened with patients resulting in staffing shortages or inadequate availability for patient education (e.g., translator, patient educator). It is imperative that providers and clinics ensure that patients understand their health condition and the long term implications of noncompliance.

Question 24
What is the primary objective of a laboratory quality management system (QMS)?

  1. Expand laboratory services
  2. Improve patient outcomes
  3. Increase laboratory efficiency
  4. Reduce laboratory costs
  5. Support evidence based decision making
Answer 24
B. Improve patient outcomes. A laboratory QMS is a comprehensive approach to ensuring that a laboratory's services are accurate, reliable and timely and that they meet the needs of patients and healthcare providers. The primary objective of a laboratory QMS is to improve patient outcomes by ensuring that laboratory testing is conducted according to established protocols, that results are accurate and reliable and that they are reported in a timely and efficient manner. Answers A, C, D and E are incorrect because while these may be important secondary objectives of a laboratory QMS, the ultimate goal is to improve patient care and outcomes.

Question 25
Which of the following quality improvement tools is best suited for identifying the underlying causes of a recurring problem in a laboratory process?

  1. Failure mode and effects analysis
  2. Flow chart
  3. Pareto chart
  4. Root cause analysis
  5. Statistical process control chart
Answer 25
D. Root cause analysis. Root cause analysis is a quality improvement tool that is widely used in the laboratory to identify the underlying causes of a recurring problem or issue. Root cause analysis involves a systematic process of investigation and problem solving, which helps to identify the fundamental reasons why a problem is occurring, rather than simply addressing the symptoms or immediate causes. Answers A, B, C and E are incorrect because while a statistical process control chart, failure mode and effects analysis, Pareto charts and flow charts are all useful quality improvement tools, root cause analysis is specifically designed to help identify and address the underlying causes of recurring problems in a laboratory process.

Question 26


Mandatory internal quality assurance (QA) mechanisms in the cytology laboratory include which one of the following procedures?

  1. Correlational QA and review of follow up surgical pathology on all cases diagnosed as low grade dysplasia (LSIL) by cytology
  2. Gynecologic Pap proficiency testing (PT)
  3. Retrospective review of previously negative Pap slides within the past 5 years on an index patient whenever a new high grade squamous intraepithelial lesion (HSIL) or malignant cervical / vaginal cytology is reported

Answer 26
C. Retrospective review of previously negative Pap slides received within the past 5 years whenever a new HSIL or malignant cervical / vaginal cytology is reported. Thorough rescreening of all (100%) negative Paps prior to sign out is neither required nor practical. Answer B is incorrect because gynecologic Pap proficiency testing is a mandatory external QA mechanism. Answer A is incorrect because correlational QA and review of follow up surgical pathology on all cases diagnosed as high grade dysplasia (HSIL) or higher by cytology is required. Correlational QA and review of low grade dysplasia with follow up surgical pathology diagnosis is not required.

Question 27
What is the ASC/SIL ratio from the following data collected from the cytology laboratory in a tertiary hospital?

AGC (%) 0.2%
ASCUS (%) 10.2%
ASC-H (%) 0.4%
LSIL (%) 4.4%
HSIL (%) 0.4%
Malignant (%) 0.1%

  1. 1
  2. 2.1
  3. 2.3
  4. 25.5
Answer 27
B. 2.1.
