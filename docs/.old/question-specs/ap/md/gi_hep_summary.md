## Strategic Notes

### **High-Yield AR Topics Prioritized**
- **GIST** and **gastric NETs** (AR) prioritized over some routine benign entities due to clinical significance
- **Viral hepatitis** and **autoimmune hepatitis** (AR) essential despite designation
- **Cholangiocarcinoma** and **pancreatic NETs** critical for practice

### **Educational Efficiency Through Combinations**
- **Barrett esophagus** combines metaplasia and dysplasia assessment
- **IBD differential** maximizes learning by contrasting UC vs. Crohn
- **Polyp differentials** test multiple classification systems simultaneously
- **Steatohepatitis** combines alcoholic and NASH patterns

### **Major Exclusions and Rationale**
- **Normal anatomy/histology** - excluded for practical focus
- **Lymphomas** - belong in hematopathology section
- **Rare infectious agents** - limited clinical relevance
- **Technical procedures** - not diagnostic skills
- **Very rare tumors** (acinar cell carcinoma, pancreatoblastoma) - space constraints

### **Clinical Relevance Emphasis**
- **85% malignant or premalignant entities** reflecting exam importance
- **Major differentials** (dysplasia vs. carcinoma, benign vs. malignant) emphasized
- **Biomarker interpretation** and grading systems prioritized
- **Treatment-related changes** and complications included where high-yield

### **Balanced Distribution Strategy**
- **GI tract** receives largest allocation (52%) matching content specifications
- **Liver pathology** heavily weighted toward inflammatory and neoplastic conditions
- **Pancreatic section** focuses on most clinically significant entities despite limited space
