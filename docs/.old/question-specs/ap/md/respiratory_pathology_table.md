## Strategic Notes

### Major Inclusions & Rationale
- **Prioritized all major lung carcinomas** (squamous, adenocarcinoma, small cell, large cell NE) as these represent the highest-yield topics
- **Emphasized critical differentials** that practicing pathologists encounter regularly (mesothelioma vs. adenocarcinoma, carcinoid vs. atypical carcinoid)
- **Included key inflammatory conditions** with distinct morphology (sarcoidosis, DAD, BOOP, UIP/NSIP)
- **Combined related topics** for educational efficiency (e.g., "Small cell vs. large cell NE differential" rather than separate questions)

### Major Exclusions & Rationale
- **Normal anatomy/histology** - standard exclusions per guidelines
- **Technical procedures** - not relevant for morphologic competency
- **Very rare entities** - PEComa, lymphoepithelial-like carcinoma prioritized lower due to rarity
- **Some inflammatory conditions** - follicular bronchitis, ABPA excluded as lower clinical yield
- **Basic pleural conditions** - pleuritis/pleural plaques excluded as straightforward diagnosis

### AR Topic Prioritization Over Core
- **Mesothelioma [AR]** prioritized over some core topics due to critical diagnostic importance
- **Interstitial pneumonia patterns [AR]** essential for pulmonary pathology competency
- **Sarcomatoid carcinoma [AR]** important differential diagnosis challenge
- **Thymoma classification [AR]** high-yield mediastinal pathology

### Educational Efficiency Strategies
- **Combined carcinoid tumors** into single differential question
- **Grouped mesothelioma topics** to cover both subtyping and differential diagnosis
- **Merged inflammatory patterns** where morphologically related
- **Focused on pattern recognition** rather than pure memorization

### Difficulty Distribution Rationale
- **Emphasized medium difficulty** (68%) to match resident competency expectations
- **Limited easy questions** to one obvious diagnosis (hamartoma)
- **Concentrated hard questions** on critical differentials that significantly impact patient care
- **Avoided extremely rare diagnoses** that would artificially inflate difficulty
