## Strategic Notes

### **Thyroid-Heavy Allocation (50%)**
- **Thyroid receives half the questions** due to being the largest subsection (18/49 topics) and highest clinical relevance
- **Major carcinoma types** (papillary, follicular, medullary) prioritized as Core competencies
- **Advanced carcinoma types** (poorly differentiated, anaplastic) included despite AR designation due to clinical significance
- **Key differential** (follicular adenoma vs. carcinoma) emphasized as frequently tested

### **High-Yield AR Topics Prioritized**
- **Poorly differentiated thyroid carcinoma** (AR) prioritized over some benign entities due to prognostic importance
- **Neuroblastoma** (AR) included as critical pediatric tumor
- **Anaplastic thyroid carcinoma** (AR) included due to aggressive nature and diagnostic importance

### **Critical Differentials Emphasized**
- **Follicular adenoma vs. carcinoma** - classic diagnostic challenge requiring capsular/vascular invasion assessment
- **Adrenocortical adenoma vs. carcinoma** - important for staging and prognosis
- **Paraganglioma vs. pheochromocytoma** - location and clinical correlation critical
- **Parathyroid adenoma vs. hyperplasia** - fundamental endocrine pathology concept

### **Major Exclusions and Rationale**
- **Normal anatomy/histology** - excluded per standard protocol
- **Most infectious conditions** - low clinical yield in endocrine pathology
- **Hypophysitis** and rare inflammatory conditions - space constraints
- **Parathyroid carcinoma** - extremely rare, space better used elsewhere
- **Technical/processing topics** - not diagnostic skills

### **Clinical Relevance Focus**
- **80% malignant or premalignant entities** reflecting exam emphasis
- **Biomarker interpretation** (calcitonin, CEA, chromogranin) incorporated
- **Prognostic grading systems** emphasized where applicable
- **MEN syndromes** included for syndrome recognition despite AR designation

### **Balanced Approach**
- **Even Core/AR distribution** (50% Core, 50% AR/Combined) reflecting clinical importance over pure designation
- **Emphasis on medium difficulty** (65%) - appropriate for endocrine complexity
- **Zero low-yield questions** - maximum educational efficiency
- **Strong focus on neoplastic pathology** (75% of questions) matching exam patterns
