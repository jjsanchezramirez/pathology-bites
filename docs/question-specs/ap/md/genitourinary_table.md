## Strategic Notes
- **Heavy emphasis on renal cell carcinoma subtypes** and their differentials as these are frequently encountered and tested
- **Urothelial carcinoma variants prioritized** due to clinical importance and diagnostic challenges
- **Reactive vs. neoplastic differentials** emphasized (BCG effects vs. CIS, nephrogenic metaplasia vs. adenocarcinoma)
- **Advanced resident topics weighted heavily** when they represent common diagnostic challenges in practice
- **Excluded very rare entities** (syringomatous adenoma, clear cell adenocarcinoma) to focus on high-yield content
- **Combined morphologically similar lesions** into differential diagnosis questions for educational efficiency
- **Processing and technical aspects included** as these are practically important for residents
