## Strategic Notes

### **Ovarian-Heavy Allocation (37%)**
- **Ovaries receive the largest allocation** due to having the most complex tumor classification system
- **All major tumor types covered**: surface epithelial-stromal (serous, mucinous, clear cell, endometrioid), sex cord-stromal (granulosa cell, Sertoli-Leydig), and germ cell tumors (dysgerminoma, teratoma, yolk sac)
- **Critical differentials emphasized**: primary vs. metastatic mucinous tumors, benign vs. borderline vs. malignant serous tumors

### **High-Yield AR Topics Prioritized**
- **Cervical adenocarcinoma in situ** (AR) prioritized due to increasing incidence and diagnostic importance
- **High-grade serous carcinoma** (AR) included due to significance in current understanding of ovarian cancer origins
- **Endometrial stromal sarcoma** (AR) essential for uterine sarcoma recognition

### **Crucial Differentials Emphasized**
- **Cervical precancers and invasive lesions** - fundamental gynecologic pathology
- **Endometrial hyperplasia vs. carcinoma** - critical for management decisions
- **Leiomyoma vs. leiomyosarcoma** - classic diagnostic challenge
- **Primary vs. metastatic ovarian tumors** - frequently tested differential

### **Major Exclusions and Rationale**
- **Normal anatomy/histology** - excluded per standard protocol
- **Most infectious conditions** - lower clinical yield in gynecologic pathology
- **Lymphomas** - belong in hematopathology section
- **Very rare tumors** (sex cord tumor with annular tubules, embryonal carcinoma) - space constraints
- **Technical procedures** - not diagnostic skills

### **Educational Efficiency Through Combinations**
- **Cervical SIL** combines LSIL and HSIL recognition in single question
- **Ovarian tumor spectrums** test multiple classification levels simultaneously
- **Endometriosis recognition** spans multiple anatomic sites

### **Clinical Relevance Focus**
- **80% malignant or premalignant entities** reflecting exam importance
- **HPV-related lesions** heavily weighted (cervical and vulvar SIL)
- **Hormone-related conditions** included (PCOS, Arias-Stella reaction)
- **Pregnancy-related pathology** represented (ectopic pregnancy, clear cell carcinoma)

### **Balanced Coverage Strategy**
- **Even Core/AR distribution** (54% Core, 46% AR) reflecting clinical importance
- **Medium difficulty emphasis** (74%) appropriate for complex gynecologic pathology
- **Comprehensive anatomic coverage** ensuring no major organ system neglected
- **Strong focus on neoplastic pathology** (77% of questions) matching exam patterns
